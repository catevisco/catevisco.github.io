HIV in Southeast Asia - A portrait


In Asia HIV has arrived almost ten years later than in the West, by the early 1990s. In 1992 699,000 people in Southeast Asia were infected with HIV.Since then it has rapidily spread in the area. In South and South-East Asia, an estimated 4 million [3.1 million–4.6 million] people were living with HIV in 2011, compared to 3.7 million [3.2 million–5.1 million] in 2001. Each country is facing a different situation and different challenges. Some, like Thailand are adopting strageties that during the past years have successfully reduce the rate of new infections. Others are still failing in containing the epidemics. This is a portrait of that region known with the name of South East Asia except for one country, Singapore, for which I have not found enough and significant data.
